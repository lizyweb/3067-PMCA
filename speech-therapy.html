Speech therapy plays a vital role in the development and success of children with special needs in our school. Through tailored programs and specialized interventions, our speech therapy services aim to address communication challenges and empower students to reach their full potential.

Enhancing Communication Skills: Speech therapy helps children improve their ability to communicate effectively, whether through speech, language, or alternative communication methods.

Building Confidence: By addressing communication difficulties, speech therapy boosts children's confidence and self-esteem, enabling them to participate more fully in social interactions and academic activities.

Supporting Academic Success: Improved communication skills contribute to better academic performance, as students can more effectively express their thoughts, understand instructions, and engage with classroom material.

Speech therapy is a fundamental component of our holistic approach to supporting the diverse needs of special kids in our school.

Assessment: Each student undergoes a comprehensive speech and language assessment to identify their specific strengths and areas of need.

Individualized Treatment Plans: Based on assessment results, personalized treatment plans are developed to target specific communication goals and objectives.

Therapeutic Techniques: Speech therapists employ a variety of evidence-based techniques and strategies to address speech and language challenges, including articulation exercises, language drills, and social communication skills training.

Through individualized programs, collaborative efforts, and a commitment to ongoing progress monitoring, we empower students to overcome communication barriers and thrive in both academic and social contexts.

Articulation: Helping students produce speech sounds correctly to improve clarity and intelligibility.

Language Development: Supporting the development of vocabulary, grammar, comprehension, and expressive language skills.

Fluency: Assisting students who stutter or exhibit other fluency disorders in achieving smoother speech patterns.

Voice Disorders: Addressing issues related to pitch, volume, quality, and resonance of voice.

Pragmatic Skills: Teaching social communication skills such as turn-taking, topic maintenance, and nonverbal communication.

Speech Therapy Sessions

1.Articulation Therapy

Phonological Awareness: Students learn to recognize and manipulate the sounds of language through activities such as rhyming, blending, and segmenting.
Speech Sound Production: Techniques such as oral motor exercises, auditory discrimination, and repetition are used to improve the accuracy of speech sound production.

2.Language Therapy

Vocabulary Development: Students expand their vocabulary through activities such as categorization, word associations, and semantic mapping.
Grammar and Syntax: Structured exercises and games help students learn and practice grammatical rules and sentence structure.

3.Social Communication Skills

Conversational Turn-taking: Role-playing and interactive activities help students learn the rules of conversation, including initiating, maintaining, and ending interactions.
Pragmatic Language: Students learn appropriate social language skills, such as making requests, giving compliments, and expressing empathy.

4.Fluency Therapy

Stuttering Modification: Techniques such as easy onset, pausing, and desensitization are used to reduce the frequency and severity of stuttering.
Fluency Shaping: Strategies to improve overall fluency, such as slow and smooth speech, breathing techniques, and phrasing, are taught.
